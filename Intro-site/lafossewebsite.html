Hello! My name is Cass (he/they). I'm looking forward to getting to know you all better, but in the meantime, here's a little about me.

I am a strategy and communications professional with a background in journalism, public relations, and corporate advocacy. I have worked in and with a variety of organisations, from global media companies to grassroots campaign groups, and have experience in sectors from finance and technology, to advertising and human rights.

My area of expertise is in business culture and change management: I help my clients to develop and implement programmes that strengthen their employee voice structures and public posture on social justice issues. I am particularly interested in the societal role and responsibility of business, and how companies can use their influence to drive meaningful positive change.

Besides my work, I'm a gamer and huge fantasy/sci-fi dork, with a penchant for RPGs and a very, very large pile of books to read - and one to write - this year!

Skills

I've built a range of skills over my career, including:
  • Strategic communications: I'm well versed in building strategic messaging that compels and engages audiences on issues like CSR, responsible journalism and hate crime.
  • Research: my first professional role was as a researcher in investigative journalism, and since then I've applied that investigative capability to my client work.
  • Writing & editing: my success so far has hinged on my ability to express myself in word and speech, as well as to communicate complex topics to a diversity of audiences.
  • Project management: most of my work has been project-based, and I've completed extensive projects to the satisfaction of clients like the Wellcome Trust, PepsiCo, Hitachi and McLaren.
  • Consultancy & advisory: I'm a fixer of problems, able to take in the full picture of a nuanced challenge - its risks and opportunities - and provide judged advice on addressing root causes and end consequences.
  • Public speaking: as the image hopefully illustrates, I've been able to leverage background in theatre to become a confident public speaker, interviewer and presenter.
  • Advocacy & campaigning: some of the key attributes needed to be an effective advocate - coalition-building, making a lot from a little, resilience to oppoisition and setbacks.
  • Leadership: I manage teams and projects like a scrum master, serving as architect, mentor and therapist; my focus is on facilitating collaboration, addressing risks and issues while providing the team with an overarching vision of and approach to the work.
  • Client engagement: my clients have gone on record to describe me as "collaborative, hard-working, reliable, caring and humorous", with "superior client management" and "excellent listening and facilitation skills".
